BPE INSPECTION — why removing quotes saves almost nothing
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  'rule="causal"'
    5 tokens: ['rule', '="', 'ca', 'usal', '"']

  'rule=causal'
    4 tokens: ['rule', '=', 'ca', 'usal']

  ', confidence=0.95'
    6 tokens: [',', ' confidence', '=', '0', '.', '95']

  'lesson="Testing 模式 OAuth 7 天过期"'
    14 tokens: ['lesson', '="', 'Testing', ' �', '��', '式', ' OAuth', ' ', '7', ' �', '�', '过', '期', '"']

  'lesson=Testing 模式 OAuth 7 天过期'
    13 tokens: ['lesson', '=', 'Testing', ' �', '��', '式', ' OAuth', ' ', '7', ' �', '�', '过', '期']

Key finding: BPE has merged `="` into a single token (the pattern is
  ubiquitous in JSON/Python/JS training data). Removing the opening
  quote saves zero tokens because there is no separate `"` token to
  drop — it was already part of `="`.
